# Minor Arcana - Suit of Cups

**Element:** Water | **Domain:** Emotions, relationships, intuition, creativity, the heart, dreams

The Cups represent our emotional world—love, relationships, feelings, intuition, and creative expression that flows from the heart. They govern how we connect with others and ourselves emotionally. When many Cups appear, the reading concerns matters of the heart, relationships, and emotional fulfillment.

---

## Ace of Cups
**Keywords:** New love, emotional beginning, compassion, creativity, intuition

**Upright:** A new emotional beginning overflows with possibility. New love, deepened compassion, creative inspiration, or spiritual awakening may be arriving. The heart opens; feelings flow freely. This is an invitation to receive and give love, to let emotions move through you. Say yes to the heart.

**Reversed:** Blocked emotions, emptiness, repressed feelings. The cup is offered but can't be received. The seeker may be emotionally closed, struggling with self-love, or unable to accept affection. Creative or spiritual channels feel blocked. Ask: What's preventing you from receiving love?

---

## Two of Cups
**Keywords:** Partnership, unity, mutual attraction, connection, harmony

**Upright:** A beautiful meeting of hearts. This card represents mutual attraction, partnership, and harmonious connection—romantic or otherwise. Two people see and honor each other. Relationships begin or deepen; agreements are made in good faith. The union is balanced and reciprocal.

**Reversed:** Imbalance, breakup, lack of harmony. The partnership is out of sync—one gives more than the other, or the connection is breaking down. Self-love may be lacking, affecting ability to partner. Miscommunication damages connection. Ask: Is this relationship reciprocal? What's out of balance?

---

## Three of Cups
**Keywords:** Celebration, friendship, community, collaboration, joy

**Upright:** Joyful celebration with beloved friends. This card represents friendship, community support, and shared happiness. Gatherings, parties, and collaborative creativity are indicated. There's abundance in connection—the seeker is supported and celebrated by their people.

**Reversed:** Overindulgence, gossip, isolation from friends. Celebration becomes excess; friendship sours into drama. The seeker may feel excluded from community or be isolating themselves. Group dynamics may be toxic. Ask: Are your friendships nourishing or draining?

---

## Four of Cups
**Keywords:** Apathy, contemplation, disconnection, reevaluation, discontent

**Upright:** Emotional withdrawal and contemplation. The seeker may be bored, discontent, or too focused on what's missing to see what's offered. There's a cup being extended—an opportunity—but it goes unnoticed. This card calls for examining the source of dissatisfaction before dismissing available gifts.

**Reversed:** Renewed interest, acceptance, moving forward. The seeker begins to see opportunities they'd been missing, or finally processes emotions they'd been avoiding. Emerging from withdrawal. Sometimes indicates deeper depression. Ask: What opportunity are you overlooking?

---

## Five of Cups
**Keywords:** Loss, grief, disappointment, regret, focusing on the negative

**Upright:** Grief and disappointment over what's been lost. Three cups have spilled—this loss is real and deserves mourning. Yet two cups remain standing behind the figure; not all is lost. This card honors grief while gently pointing toward what remains. The bridge home still stands.

**Reversed:** Acceptance, moving forward, healing. The seeker begins to turn around, to see what remains rather than only what's lost. Healing from grief progresses. Sometimes indicates prolonged grief or refusing to acknowledge loss. Ask: What remains that you haven't yet seen?

---

## Six of Cups
**Keywords:** Nostalgia, childhood, innocence, past connections, gifts

**Upright:** Sweet nostalgia and the healing power of innocence. Past connections resurface—childhood friends, old homes, memories. There's an invitation to reconnect with simpler times and childlike joy. Gifts are given with pure intention. The past offers something valuable to the present.

**Reversed:** Living in the past, stuck in nostalgia, childhood wounds. The seeker may be romanticizing the past at the expense of the present, or childhood wounds are affecting adult life. Naive thinking may be problematic. Ask: Is the past nourishing your present or trapping you?

---

## Seven of Cups
**Keywords:** Illusion, fantasy, choices, wishful thinking, temptation

**Upright:** Many options appear, but which are real? This card represents the realm of fantasy, imagination, and wishful thinking. The seeker faces multiple possibilities but may be seduced by illusions. Daydreaming is pleasant but clarity is needed before choosing. Ground fantasies in reality.

**Reversed:** Clarity, making a choice, illusions dispelled. The fog lifts; the seeker sees clearly what's real and chooses accordingly. Alternatively, someone may be deceiving themselves or being deceived. Escapism may be problematic. Ask: Which vision is real? What are you avoiding through fantasy?

---

## Eight of Cups
**Keywords:** Departure, leaving behind, seeking deeper meaning, disillusionment

**Upright:** Walking away from what no longer fulfills. Despite having built something (eight cups stacked), the seeker turns toward the unknown, seeking deeper meaning. This is not abandonment but purposeful departure—the soul knows something more awaits. A difficult but necessary journey.

**Reversed:** Fear of leaving, staying in unfulfilling situations, aimless wandering. The seeker knows they need to leave but can't, or has left but without clear direction. Avoiding necessary emotional journeys. Ask: What are you staying in that's already empty? Where are you running to?

---

## Nine of Cups
**Keywords:** Wish fulfillment, contentment, satisfaction, gratitude, emotional abundance

**Upright:** The "wish card." Emotional satisfaction and dreams coming true. The seeker may be experiencing or approaching a time of contentment and abundance. Take time to appreciate what you have; gratitude amplifies joy. This is a moment of genuine happiness.

**Reversed:** Dissatisfaction despite having much, materialism, smugness. The seeker may be chasing external markers of success while feeling empty inside, or may be complacent and smug. Wishes granted don't bring expected fulfillment. Ask: What would truly satisfy you? Are you grateful for what you have?

---

## Ten of Cups
**Keywords:** Harmony, family, emotional fulfillment, happiness, alignment

**Upright:** Emotional paradise—the happy family under the rainbow. This card represents the fulfillment of emotional dreams: loving relationships, family harmony, lasting happiness. The full arc of emotional development culminates in shared joy. This is what the heart truly wants.

**Reversed:** Disconnection, dysfunctional family, broken dreams. The ideal isn't matching reality. Family or relationship problems disrupt harmony. The seeker may be chasing an unrealistic ideal or suffering from genuinely troubled connections. Ask: Is your ideal realistic? What's disrupting harmony?

---

## Page of Cups
**Keywords:** Creative opportunity, intuition, messenger, inner child, dreams

**Upright:** A gentle messenger of emotional or creative opportunity. The Page of Cups brings invitations to explore feelings, develop intuition, or pursue creative dreams. As a person, they're sensitive, imaginative, possibly dreamy. Pay attention to dreams, intuitions, and creative promptings.

**Reversed:** Emotional immaturity, creative blocks, ignoring intuition. The seeker may be avoiding emotional growth, blocked creatively, or dismissing intuitive messages. A message may be disappointing or delayed. Ask: What creative or emotional invitation are you ignoring?

---

## Knight of Cups
**Keywords:** Romance, charm, imagination, following the heart, artistic pursuit

**Upright:** The romantic quester, following the heart's calling. This Knight pursues dreams, offers love gracefully, and leads with emotion. As a person, they're charming, artistic, romantic, possibly impractical. As energy, follow your heart; pursue the beautiful and meaningful.

**Reversed:** Moodiness, unrealistic expectations, emotional manipulation. The Knight's romance becomes manipulation; imagination becomes escapism. The seeker may be chasing impossible dreams or dealing with someone charming but unreliable. Ask: Is this romance or fantasy? Are feelings being weaponized?

---

## Queen of Cups
**Keywords:** Compassion, intuition, emotional security, nurturing, psychic ability

**Upright:** Deep emotional wisdom and compassionate presence. The Queen of Cups holds her cup closed—she contains and masters her emotions rather than being mastered by them. As a person, she's intuitive, nurturing, possibly psychic. As energy, trust your emotional intelligence; offer compassion.

**Reversed:** Emotional insecurity, codependency, blocked intuition. The Queen's depths become murky—she may be overwhelmed by emotion, giving too much, or disconnected from intuition. Boundaries may be lacking. Ask: Are you nurturing yourself? Have you lost yourself in others' emotions?

---

## King of Cups
**Keywords:** Emotional balance, diplomacy, composure, wise counsel, control

**Upright:** Mastery of the emotional realm. The King of Cups sits stable on turbulent waters—he feels deeply but isn't ruled by feelings. As a person, he's emotionally intelligent, diplomatic, a wise counselor. As energy, balance head and heart; respond rather than react.

**Reversed:** Emotional manipulation, volatility, coldness. The King's composure cracks—he may become emotionally manipulative, explosively moody, or coldly suppressed. The seeker may be dealing with such a person or struggling with their own emotional control. Ask: Are you controlling emotions or suppressing them?
